Networking Media

  • Wired Media:
    • Twisted-pair cable: Commonly used for telephone communication and Ethernet networks. It contains copper wire and creates a magnetic field around it.
    • Coaxial cable: Also used in wired networks.
    • Fiber-optic cable: A glass or plastic conductor that carries signals as light. It offers high bandwidth and is more flexible than copper cable.
  • Wireless Media:
    • Uses patterns of electromagnetic waves for data transfer.
    • Examples include radio waves in wireless data communications networks.
