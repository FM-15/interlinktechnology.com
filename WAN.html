Wide Area Network (WAN)

A wide area network is a telecommunications network that extends over a large geographic area. It is a collection of LANs or other networks that communicate with one another. WAN connections can be wired or wireless, even though they cover a large area. Wireless WANs include 4G/5G and Long-Term Evolution (LTE) networks. At the same time, wired WANs typically include broadband internet services and Multiprotocol Label Switching (MPLS), to increase the speed.The internet is the world's biggest WAN.

Advantages:

  • Wide Area Network (WAN) technology allows seamless communication across different nations or continents. It ensures fast message delivery while maintaining security and anonymity.
  • With WAN, you can swiftly download updated files and data from the server, ensuring that all connected devices have access to the latest information.
  • Business people can sell their items online not only in their city, state, or nation but also in foreign nations using WAN networks.
  • Since WANs have a wider coverage area than other networks, their bandwidth is also higher than other networks.

Disadvantages:

  • WAN faces more security concerns due to interconnected technologies, potentially inviting malicious attacks and identity theft.
  • Setting up WANs is complex and expensive, requiring routers, switches, and security tools.
  • Some remote areas lack energy supply or proper infrastructure, leading to frequent disconnections. Dedicated ISP lines can mitigate this issue.
  • Identifying network problems in extensive WANs is time-consuming, especially when buried under the ocean. Repairs are resource-intensive.
  • Maintaining a 24/7 data center for WANs is challenging, requiring constant support from network technicians and administrators.
