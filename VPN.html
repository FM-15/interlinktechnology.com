Virtual Private Network (VPN)

A VPN is a type of technology used to establish a secure network connection when using public networks. All data transmitted between your device and the VPN server is encrypted, making it more difficult for third parties to monitor your online activities and pilfer sensitive information. It prevents unauthorized individuals from eavesdropping on traffic and enables the user to conduct remote work. VPNs conceal your internet protocol (IP) address, making your online activities practically anonymous. Most importantly, VPN services provide private and encrypted connections that provide more privacy than even a protected Wi-Fi hotspot.

Advantages:

  • VPNs enhance online safety and privacy by masking your IP address, location, and browsing activity, preventing tracking by websites, ISPs, and others.
  • VPNs allow anonymous internet use by concealing your identity, protecting against tracking by websites, browsers, and cable companies.

Disadvantages:

  • VPNs add encryption and rerouting, which can slow down internet performance and consume data.
  • VPN privacy depends on the provider's trustworthiness. Some log user activities, so research reputable providers with transparent policies.
  • VPNs are legal in many regions, some countries ban or regulate them.
  • Some free VPNs monetize user data. Investigate providers' business models to protect your data.
