Networking Devices

Networking devices are physical devices that allow hardware on a computer network to communicate and interact with one another. A few networking devices are mentioned below:

Router:Routes data between different networks (e.g., LAN and WAN) and manages IP addresses.

Switch:Connects devices within a local network (LAN) by forwarding data based on MAC addresses.

Hub:It is a basic device that connects multiple devices in a network, but it lacks intelligence.

Access Point (AP):Enables wireless connectivity by providing Wi-Fi access to devices.

Firewall:Protects networks by filtering incoming and outgoing traffic based on predefined rules.

Modem: Converts digital data from a computer into analog signals for transmission over telephone lines or cable lines.

Network Interface Card (NIC): Hardware that allows devices to connect to a network (wired or wireless).
